ABOUT LAMP

In some cultural and religious traditions, particularly in Hinduism, lighting a lamp (like a diya or oil lamp) is a practice associated with death and the afterlife. This practice is believed to guide the soul on its journey and provide light and guidance to the deceased.

Here's a more detailed explanation:

Symbolism:

Lighting a lamp symbolizes the soul's journey towards light and liberation.

Ritual Practice:

In Hindu funeral rituals, a diya is lit near the body as a way to honor the deceased and guide their soul.

Duration:

In some traditions, a lamp is kept lit for a specific period, such as 13 days, after a person's death, according to a Facebook post. This is believed to be a time of transition for the soul.

Other Practices:

Offerings like rice, water, and flowers are also made during these rituals.

Variations:

Practices may vary by family and region.
