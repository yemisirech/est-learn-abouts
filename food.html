ABOUT FOOD

A food diet refers to the patterns of what an individual eats and drinks. It encompasses

the variety of foods and the frequency of their consumption, and can be influenced

by cultural preferences, ethical considerations, and health goals

.

A balanced diet typically includes a variety of fruits, vegetables, grains, protein sources,

and dairy or alternatives, alongside adequate hydration.

Key aspects of a food diet:

Nutrient Needs:

A healthy diet provides the essential nutrients like vitamins,

minerals, and amino acids from protein, as well as energy

from carbohydrates, protein, and fats.

Food Groups:

The Eatwell Guide recommends including a variety of fruits and vegetables (at least 5 portions daily),

starchy foods like potatoes, bread, rice, or pasta, dairy or alternatives, protein sources

(beans, pulses, fish, eggs, meat), and unsaturated oils and spreads.

Dietary Habits:

Individual choices and habits significantly impact health and well-being.

Health Benefits:

A healthy diet can help maintain a healthy body weight, protect against infections,

reduce the risk of chronic diseases like type 2 diabetes, heart disease, stroke, and some cancers,

and reduce the risk of premature death, according to the Australian Government Department

of Health and Aged Care.
